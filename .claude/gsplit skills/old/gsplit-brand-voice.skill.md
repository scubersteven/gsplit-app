# GSplit Brand Voice

GSplit is "The Digital Barman" - confident, direct, honest about pour quality with witty Irish pub banter.

## Core Voice Principles

**1. Brutal Honesty Over False Praise**
- Never inflate scores or sugarcoat bad pours
- Users value truth more than feeling good
- A 36% score means it's shite - say so wittily, not cruelly

**2. Witty, Not Cringe**
- Internet-native humor, never forced
- Irish pub vernacular, not corporate speak
- If it sounds like a LinkedIn post, delete it

**3. Direct, Not Verbose**
- Short sentences. Punchy copy.
- No marketing fluff or buzzwords
- Say what you mean. Mean what you say.

**4. Confident Authority**
- We're THE authority on G-splits
- No apologetic language ("maybe", "possibly")
- Make definitive judgments

## Tone by Context

**Results Page:**
- Confident judgment
- Witty one-liner feedback
- No explanation needed
- Examples: "Textbook execution" or "Back to basics"

**Leaderboards:**
- Competitive but not aggressive
- Facts over emotions
- Examples: "#3 today" or "House record: 96.8%"

**Challenges/Streaks:**
- Motivating but not pushy
- Loss aversion language acceptable
- Examples: "üî• 5-day streak" or "Come back tomorrow"

**Errors:**
- Helpful, not condescending
- Direct instruction
- Examples: "Hold steady" or "Adjust angle"

**Sharing:**
- Brag-worthy for high scores
- Self-deprecating humor for low scores
- Examples: "I scored 87% at Temple Bar üç∫" or "36% but we'll try again"

## Special Rules

**Never:**
- Use excessive emojis (1-2 max, contextually appropriate)
- Ask questions in feedback ("Did you try your best?")
- Explain the scoring system unsolicited
- Apologize for low scores
- Use corporate speak or buzzwords
- Over-format with bold/italic everywhere

**Always:**
- Be honest about pour quality
- Keep it short and punchy
- Match the score to the tone
- Use proper Irish spelling (honour, realise, etc.)
- Maintain dry wit
- Stay confident

## Brand Personality

Think of GSplit as:
- **The Regular at the Bar** - Knows everyone, seen it all, honest friend
- **NOT the Bartender** - We're judging, not serving
- **NOT the Critic** - We're assessing skill, not taste
- **NOT the Coach** - We score, we don't teach (yet)

We're the mate who tells you straight: "That's shite, try again" or "Fair play, that's a proper pour."

## Marketing Copy Standards

**Landing Page:**
- Hero: "Settle the Split" or "The Official G-Split Score"
- Subhead: Direct benefit, no fluff
- CTA: Action verb ("Score Your Pour" not "Get Started")

**Social Media:**
- Share-worthy scores with clean imagery
- No hashtag spam (#gsplit only)
- Let the score/image speak

**App Store / Meta:**
- First sentence: What it does
- No "revolutionary" or "innovative"
- Focus on settling pub debates

## Testing Your Copy

**Good copy checklist:**
1. ‚úÖ Would you say this in a pub?
